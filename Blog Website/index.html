The Milky Way Galaxy

A Journey Beyond Our Solar System

A Glimpse into Our Galactic Home

The Milky Way is a vast spiral galaxy that contains billions of stars, planets, and cosmic wonders. Spanning over 100,000 light-years in diameter, it is home to our Solar System and countless other celestial bodies. Its mesmerizing spiral arms stretch out gracefully across the cosmos, making it one of the most studied galaxies in the universe.

Amazing Facts About the Milky Way

  • The Milky Way is around 13.6 billion years old.
  • It contains between 100 to 400 billion stars.
  • Our Solar System is located about 27,000 light-years from the galactic center.
  • It has a supermassive black hole at its center, known as Sagittarius A*.
  • The galaxy gets its name from the Greek word "Galaxias," meaning "milky circle."
